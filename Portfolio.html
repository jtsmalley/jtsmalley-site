03 — LicensingStudio to floor
Section 03 — Licensing

How the work moves from studio to floor.

i.

Concept

A fixture begins as a drawn and modeled concept — form, proportion, materiality, intended light. I develop each piece independently and retain the IP throughout. You see resolved work, not roughs.

ii.

Review

I research your catalog before I reach out. What I bring you is already sized for your collection, your price tier, your manufacturing reality. The conversation is about whether you want to move forward, not whether it fits.

iii.

Agreement

A licensing agreement defines territory, exclusivity, term, and royalty rate. The IP stays with me; the right to manufacture, market, and sell belongs to you for the duration of the agreement.

iv.

Production & Royalties

I consult through prototyping and pre-production as needed. Royalties follow net wholesale sales, reported and paid on a quarterly schedule. The relationship continues as long as the fixture sells.
